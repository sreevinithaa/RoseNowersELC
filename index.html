About Us

Rose Nowers History

Rose Nowers (nee-Clark) was the daughter of Laurie and Catherine Clark and grew up within the Port Headland Region.Rose was a trained nurse and during the war years travelled to Perth wanting to join the armed Services.However, she was an Aboriginal womanRose was always involved in human right issues for people in general.In the early forties a large community meeting was arranged for Aboriginal people in the hall in Port Hedland.Son Mcleod addressed this meeting and Rose was so exited that she forgot to take the minutes of the meeting.

Rose later married Tom Nowers and had a large family.Rose's community involvement was widespread and did not just involve Aboriginal people.She was involved with many community areas and organisations such as the aged people's home and helping start the Aboriginal progress association.Rose was a member of the Pundulmurra Advisory Committee during the '70's and she was the only Aboriginal women on the Hedland College Streering Committee when the groundwork was being done. Rosse Nowers was a hard worker for the benefit of all people in the community - her one regret in life was that the Clark family was never given the recognition it deserved in Port Hedland as she followed in the footsteps of a great man...her father.

Rose Nowers Philosophy

Rose Nowers Early Learning Centre acknowledges Kariyarra people as the traditional custodians of the land on which we walk and respects the elders both past, present and emerging

We honour, value and respect the diversity of aboriginal and torres strait islander cultures and all cultures reflective of our community and surrounds.Rose Nowers endeavours to enrich children's awareness of the environment and promote a sense of connection to the natural world around us.encouraging strong links to our immediate community as we believe we are strong when we are diverse

Rose Nowers values early childhood education as a foundation for lifelong learning. We see every child as a unique individual,who should be viewed, respected and treaded as such. We strive to provide holistic support for each cheld's individual requirements and interest in a nurturing, inclusive environment that fosters the child's growth throughout their learning jurney

As Educators we recognise that learning can happen anywhere, anytime; and as such our practice and curriculum reflects play based experiences as well as intentional teaching to scaffold children's learning opportunities

Holistic learning occurs as individuals work in partnership, respecting each other's values and beliefs.We aim to achieve this through positive mutual relationships and interations with families, professionals, the wider community and the natural environment

This document, out philosophy, weaves together the values, beliefs and goals that represent who we are and who we want to become

BE NICE TO OUR SELVES * BE KIND TO OUR FRIENDS * BE GENTLE WITH THE ENVIRONMENT
